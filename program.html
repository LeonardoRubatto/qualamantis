Axis Three · Cost Improvement Program

A stand-alone engine
for product cost

The Cost Improvement Program is activatable even without a performance problem. It covers product cost across all business processes — with CoNQ as one of its diagnostic instruments, converting quality losses into improvement targets.

CIP — The Engine

A structured program covering product cost across all processes. Run by a co-located, cross-functional team with a mandate to act — fuelled by competitor benchmarking and supplier workshops.

CoNQ — One of its tools

The Cost of Non-Quality measures the financial weight of quality failures — complaints, scrap and rework — and converts them into prioritised improvement targets inside the CIP.

Three levers — and the question each answers

No. 1

Product specifications

"Do my products have the right features?"

Prioritise what customers truly value — and stop paying for what they don't. Using the House of Quality and customer value mapping to align specifications with willingness to pay.

No. 2

Sourcing & price

"Am I buying at the right price?"

Clean-sheet target costing, part by part. Fuelled by supplier workshops, benchmarking and should-cost analysis — identifying the delta between current price and achievable price.

No. 3

Technical solutions

"Is there a lower-cost design?"

Teardown-driven redesign of product and process, informed by competitor benchmarking and Robust Design. Design for Six Sigma to build quality in — so you don't inspect it in at cost.

How it works in practice

⟳

A co-located, cross-functional team

Engineering, purchasing, manufacturing, sales and quality — working to shared stretch targets under one roof. Not a task-force that meets once a month.

★

Clear top-management commitment

Quick decisions, resources allocated, barriers removed. The program needs a sponsor with authority — we help you structure that commitment from day one.

◆

Dedicated resources & gate reviews

Around two FTE, a shared budget and structured gate reviews. Savings are not claimed until implemented — the gate process keeps the program honest.
